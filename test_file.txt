The sun dips low, painting the sky,
Over the pulse of Accra's humming streets.
The heavy heat begins to sigh,
Making way for slower, steadier beats.

A flick of a switch, a glowing red light,
The amplifier wakes from its silent sleep.
A quiet crackle cuts through the night,
Before the bass line settles, resonant and deep.

It’s the sound of soul, stretched out and new,
Alternative rhythms that bend and sway.
Chords like honey, dripping right through
The lingering dust of a long, loud day.

I sit beside the speaker, close and still,
A silent witness to the layered sound.
Catching the riffs, the vocal trill,
Where every hidden harmony is found.

Like a quiet observer, resting right there,
Feeling the vibrations of the wood and wire.
Translating the melodies pulled from the air,
And capturing the sparks of creative fire.

The smooth vocals drift, the synthesizer hums,
A steady symphony in the dimming light.
Until the final, fading echo comes,
And leaves a perfect stillness in the night.
